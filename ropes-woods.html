Ropes Course Woods

Where the forest becomes infrastructure — home to the school's outdoor ropes course and a space where ecological and adventure education intersect.

Overview

Forest as Classroom

Ropes Course Woods is one of the most actively used sections of the campus woodland. The school's outdoor ropes course runs through this area, integrating built structures into the existing tree canopy in a way that requires careful management of the surrounding forest.

The trees here are selected and maintained specifically to support the ropes course infrastructure, meaning this zone has a more managed character than the surrounding woods. Despite this, the ecological baseline of the area remains intact, with native woodland species present throughout.

The intersection of adventure education and natural habitat makes Ropes Course Woods an interesting case study in how human use shapes forest ecology — a theme directly relevant to the school's broader environmental research projects.
