Bowl Woods

A natural bowl-shaped depression in the Lawrenceville landscape, sheltering a unique microclimate and dense woodland understory.

Overview

The Lowest Point in the Woods

Bowl Woods sits in a natural topographic depression, making it one of the most sheltered and ecologically distinct zones on campus. The bowl shape traps moisture and cool air, creating conditions that support a richer, denser understory than the surrounding forest.

The canopy here tends to be dominated by moisture-loving species, and the forest floor is particularly lush with ferns, mosses, and other shade-tolerant plants. The zone is one of the quieter, more removed sections of the campus woods — easy to miss but rewarding to explore.

As part of the school's ongoing ecological research, Bowl Woods serves as one of the primary study sites for bat population surveys, with its dense vegetation and proximity to water making it an ideal roosting and foraging habitat.
