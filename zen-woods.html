Zen Woods

A quieter, more contemplative section of the Lawrenceville forest — a place for observation, reflection, and close study of woodland life.

Overview

A Space for Stillness

Zen Woods is named for its particular quality of quiet. Positioned away from the main athletic and academic areas of campus, it offers a genuine sense of remove — a place where the noise of school life fades and the sounds of the forest take over.

The zone features a mix of mature trees and open understory, making it easier to move through than some of the denser sections. This openness also makes it an excellent area for wildlife observation, as animals can be spotted moving between the canopy and the ground layer.

Students and faculty have long used Zen Woods as an informal retreat. Its accessibility and calm character make it one of the most visited sections of the campus woodland.
