About the Woods

A Forest Within a School

Stretching across the eastern edge of the Lawrenceville campus, the school woods are more than a backdrop — they are a living classroom, a sanctuary, and an ecosystem in their own right.

From Bowl Woods to the Meadow, each named section carries its own character: dense canopy, open meadow, creek-side habitat, and forested adventure. Together they form one of the most distinctive features of the Lawrenceville landscape.

Each section of the woods has its own distinct ecology, history, and character.
01

Bowl Woods

A natural bowl-shaped depression with a sheltered microclimate and dense understory vegetation.

02

Zen Woods

A quieter section of the forest — ideal for calm walks and close observation of woodland life.

03

Ropes Course Woods

Home to the school's outdoor ropes course, where the forest becomes a space for challenge and teamwork.

04

Hamil Woods

One of the larger forested sections, featuring mature hardwoods and a rich woodland floor.

05

Meadow Woods

Where forest meets open meadow — a transitional habitat rich in edge species and seasonal wildflowers.
