Golf Course

A maintained green space on the northern edge of campus, bordering the natural woodland areas and occupying a significant portion of the Lawrenceville grounds.

Overview

Managed Landscape

The Lawrenceville golf course occupies a large section of the northern campus grounds. As a highly managed landscape, it represents a very different ecological condition from the adjacent woodland zones — the turf is maintained, water is applied, and the vegetation is carefully controlled.

Despite its managed character, the golf course is not ecologically inert. The fairways and rough areas adjacent to the woodland edges provide foraging habitat for certain bird species, and the water features associated with the course can attract wildlife from the surrounding woods.

The boundary between the golf course and the natural woodland is one of the sharpest ecological transitions on campus — a direct contrast between intensively managed and relatively undisturbed land that is useful for understanding how different management practices shape biodiversity.
